Sinigang na Hipon: A Savory Sour Classic

Sinigang is the ultimate Filipino comfort food, famous for its characteristic sour broth. Traditionally soured with tamarind (sampaloc), this version features fresh shrimp and an assortment of local vegetables, creating a perfectly balanced, refreshing meal.

The heart of a great Sinigang is the 'asim' (sourness) it should be sharp enough to make you wince slightly, but savory enough to keep you coming back for more.

- Lola Maria, Filipino Home Cook

Ingredients:

  • Shrimp: 500g (with heads on for extra flavor)
  • Tamarind Base: 1 packet (or 100g fresh tamarind)
  • Water spinach (Kang-kong): 1 bunch
  • Radish (Labanos): 1 medium, sliced
  • Long Green Chili (Siling Haba): 2 pieces

Preparation:

  1. Boil water with tomatoes and onions until softened.
  2. Add the radish and okra; simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the tamarind base and stir well to dissolve.
  4. Add the shrimp and green chilies; cook until the shrimp turn pink.
  5. Stir in the water spinach, turn off the heat, and cover for 2 minutes.

Cooking Tips:

  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce (patis) provides the essential umami saltiness.
  • 1 squeeze of calamansi can be added at the end for an extra citrusy kick.
